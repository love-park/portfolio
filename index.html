EarSketch Teachers

Introduction

EarSketch is a platform where students can learn computer science by remixing sound stems using code. Working alongside Georgia Tech's Music Technology, CEISMC supports EarSketch's K-12 curriculum and competition by developing teacher-facing curriculum resources, providing administrative support for its competition, and conducting research to determine EarSketch's efficacy in bolstering diversity and participation of K-12 students.

In the past, curriculum materials were delivered and distributed through the platform or via email; however, one of the initiatives for the website was to deliver the curriculum more easily for teachers.

In partnership with Amazon Future Engineer and YELLOW foundation, the Your Voice is Power competition features a learning experience, integrating themes of music, computer science, entrepreneurship, and social justice.

Rationale

With growing visibility and reach through the partnership with Amazon Future Engineer, the EarSketch Teachers website felt outdated to stakeholders.

Goal

To redesign the site for teachers to explore and adopt our curriculum and competition resources.

Target Audience

Primary Audience: K-12 Teachers and Students
Secondary Audience: School Administration, School Boards, Parents

Evaluation

Aimed to amplify long-term diversity and participation in CS and STEM, we regularly collected data to evaluate our project's efficacy. Our research investigated middle and high school students' interest in STEM careers after participating in the curriculum and competition. We also captured reach and engagement data to evaluate our partnership with Amazon Future Engineer.

In addition to our survey and tracking data, we conducted teacher interviews for feedback on our curriculum and competition. Participating in these interviews, I was interested in how teachers interacted and engaged with the website.

Takeaways from Teachers:

    The website layout was helpful and better compared to the text-only curriculum.
    Teachers want more resources on the website.
    Some teachers did not know the website existed and would find more communication about the teacher website useful.
